# software-quality-pack

## Skills
- `quality-attribute-model-builder` — Define a project-specific software quality model with explicit attributes, subattributes, rationale, and evaluation intent.
- `quality-weighting-reviewer` — Assign and critique relative weights across quality attributes so tradeoffs are explicit rather than accidental.
- `quality-goal-scorer` — Score current or proposed system quality goals against evidence, constraints, and stakeholder priorities.
- `technical-debt-auditor` — Identify and classify technical debt across code, architecture, tests, documentation, and process, then recommend repayment priorities.
- `maintainability-reviewer` — Review a system for maintainability risks such as coupling, complexity, weak tests, poor structure, and difficult change paths.
- `usability-quality-reviewer` — Review usability as a software quality concern using observable task quality, clarity, consistency, feedback, and accessibility cues.
- `reliability-quality-reviewer` — Review reliability risks and evidence around failure frequency, fault tolerance, recovery behavior, and operational stability.
- `process-quality-checker` — Assess whether the development process supports quality through reviews, testing, traceability, versioning, and release discipline.
- `product-vs-process-quality-splitter` — Separate product quality concerns from process quality concerns so remediation targets the right layer.
- `quality-evidence-summarizer` — Summarize the available evidence for quality claims, highlight gaps, and make confidence and residual risk explicit.
- `quality-risk-register-builder` — Create a quality-focused risk register linking weak attributes, evidence gaps, consequences, mitigations, and owners.

## Suggested flow
1. Start with `quality-attribute-model-builder`.
2. Make tradeoffs explicit with `quality-weighting-reviewer`.
3. Score goals and claims with `quality-goal-scorer`.
4. Inspect debt and maintainability issues where quality is weak.
5. Separate process and product issues, then summarize evidence and capture quality risks.
